Voss and Pip - Is This Anything?

![[VossAndPip.png]]

Meet Voss

He doesn’t remember what ordinary thing he was doing when it happened.

He remembers before; a quiet and unremarkable life, sure, but it was his. And he remembers the after; which is Pip, already perched, already watching him with the patience of a thing that has been waiting a long time and is relieved the wait is finally over. Voss didn’t go looking for this. He is still not quite sure what this is.

Voss is neat about everything. His coat, his words, the careful way he stands in a room as though he’s already located the exits and decided which one he prefers. He watches people the some people watch fires, waiting for it to inevitably get out of hand but already knowing when that will happen. He’s not unfriendly. He just seems that way because of his carefulness.

Ask him what Pip is and he will pause for a long time before responding: “I’m still working on that”.

Meet Pip

Small. Fast. Certain, in an unearned way.

He arrived without invitation nor explanation.

He finishes sentences. Not out of impatience, but because he already knows where the sentence will end, and waiting for it to come seems like a dreadful formality. He is always right about that direction, and knows that this is not entirely endearing. He has not adjusted.

Voss noticed early on that Pip has never said anything provably false. Voss has spent considerable time deciding whether this is reassuring. It is not. Something that has never been wrong is either very wise or has never been tested.

What Pip is, exactly, is a question he deflects with the efficiency of something who has heard it many times and found the asking much more interesting that the answering. Voss asks sometimes, late at night when the camp is quiet. Pip often responds that he is still working on that. Voss suspects this is true, and he is not eased by it.

Meet Voss and Pip

If asked, they’ll tell you they’re not a team. Voss will tell you as much, very carefully, with a pause before the word team like he is checking whether it fits. Pip will tell you much quicker and with more confidence and with zero elaboration. They will then immediately function as a team, and nobody will bring it up, becuase obviously they’re a team.

Voss is an Aberrant Mind Sorcerer who doesn’t so much cast as indicate, neat and deliberate in everything including violence, built around reading the room before acting and acting precisely when he does act. Pip is a familiar by ruling, and something harder to pin down by name in every other measure. Small, construct-adjacent, forward-leaning, eager, with eyes that are slightly too steady and a certainty that has never once wavered in Voss’s presence.

In combat, Voss stands at the edge and tilts the table quietly while Pip handles what needs handling. They are the most efficient two voices in the party, and the least forthcoming. And the question of which one of them is actually doing the magic is, as Voss might say, still being worked on.

THE CONCEPT

  • Full caster character built around the Ventriloquist framework – all magic visibly routes through Pip, Voss never claims it
  • Mechanically a standard Wizard (or possibly Sorcerer)
  • Every spell narrated as Pip’s action, not Voss’s
  • The long-game twist: Pip may be the actual caster and Voss may have no magic of his own

WHO THEY ARE

Voss:

  • Normal life, interrupted by Pip arriving under circumstances neither can explain
  • Dry and watchful, reads every room before speaking
  • Neat and deliberate in appearance and speech
  • Speaks in the Considerer voice – slow, visible reconsideration mid-thought, pauses that are process not hesitation
  • Reveals nothing, cares deeply, shows it sideways
  • The open question underneath him: if Pip left, what would Voss actually be?

Pip:

  • Small construct-adjacent entity, perched, fast, certain
  • Arrived without explanation, has never offered one
  • Finishes Voss’s sentences because he already knows where they’re going
  • Occasionally acts before Voss decides – always exactly right
  • Has never said anything provably false, which Voss finds unsettling
  • Unknown origin, possibly unknown even to Pip – Option D, with Option E held in reserve

THEIR RELATIONSHIP

  • Found each other by accident neither can fully explain
  • Not a partnership either of them has named out loud
  • Pip defers to Voss in direction, Voss defers to Pip in execution
  • The “you were getting there” exchange is their signature beat – use sparingly
  • The party will eventually notice Pip acts independently and start asking questions neither of them answers

THE VOICE

  • Voss: The Considerer – lower register, slow and deliberate, reconsiders mid-sentence, pivots, lands somewhere unexpected
  • Pip: The Interrupter – higher, fast and clipped, certain, no wasted words
  • The contrast between them does character work automatically
  • Sustainable for a full campaign because the pattern runs itself once established

APPEARANCE (HeroForge-ready)

Voss:

  • Medium human, lean, angular face, high cheekbones
  • Short dark hair, close to skull, clean shaved
  • Long fitted coat, charcoal or deep navy, mid-calf
  • Plain dark clothing underneath, no patterns or adornment
  • No visible weapons, hands at sides or mid-gesture
  • Practical dark boots, no flourishes
  • Expression: neutral, slight narrowing around the eyes

Pip:

  • Crow to small hawk sized, smooth-bodied construct
  • Dark polished material, faint seams, suggestion of a beak
  • Head slightly too large proportionally
  • Large round fixed eyes, amber or pale gold, faintly luminous
  • No wings or vestigial wings that don’t explain movement
  • Forward-leaning perched posture, always attentive
  • Near black with faint warm bronze undertone in certain light

SAMPLE SCENES (established)

  • Combat: Voss says “Pip, hold” mid-fight; Pip acts before Voss finishes deciding; “you were taking too long” / “that’s fair”
  • Exploration: they finish each other’s sentences toward the same wrong answer and it works anyway
  • Social: Voss routes every interaction through Pip when pressed; neither confirms nor denies the arrangement to outsiders
  • Quiet: a party member asks if Pip has feelings; Pip says he’s still deciding; Voss says nothing; that’s the answer

WHAT’S STILL OPEN

  • Race not locked – currently defaulting to human
  • Class not locked – Wizard favored, Sorcerer viable
  • Pip’s true nature: Option D operating assumption (neither knows), Option E in reserve (Pip is the actual caster)
